Patient presents with acute onset of high fever (102°F), persistent dry cough, and shortness of breath that started 3 days ago. 

Medical History:
- 45-year-old male
- No known allergies
- Non-smoker
- Hypertension controlled with medication

Current Symptoms:
- Fever (102°F/38.9°C)
- Dry cough, worse at night
- Difficulty breathing, especially on exertion
- Fatigue and body aches
- No chest pain

Physical Examination:
- Respiratory rate elevated (22 breaths/min)
- Oxygen saturation 94% on room air
- Bilateral lung crackles on auscultation
- No wheezing

Laboratory Tests:
- White blood cell count: elevated (14,000/μL)
- C-reactive protein: elevated
- Chest X-ray: bilateral infiltrates

Preliminary Diagnosis:
- Community-acquired pneumonia likely
- Viral vs. bacterial etiology to be determined

Treatment Plan:
- Empiric antibiotic therapy initiated (azithromycin)
- Supportive care with antipyretics
- Close monitoring of oxygen saturation
- Follow-up chest X-ray in 48 hours

Patient advised to rest, maintain hydration, and return if symptoms worsen or new symptoms develop (chest pain, severe difficulty breathing).
